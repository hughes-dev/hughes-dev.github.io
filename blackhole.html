This application is a 2D simulation of a black hole, illustrating the dynamic behavior of celestial objects under gravitational influence. Built with C# and Windows Presentation Foundation (WPF), the simulation provides a visual and intuitive understanding of gravitational forces and orbital dynamics in a simple yet effective manner.
The simulation creates a central black hole and a predetermined number of other celestial bodies. Each of these bodies is assigned a randomized position, velocity, and acceleration. The black hole exerts a gravitational pull on the surrounding objects, with the strength of the pull determined by a constant mimicking the gravitational constant. The objects then move according to the resulting forces, effectively demonstrating how gravitational forces lead to the creation of orbits. If any of the objects comes too close to the black hole, it's absorbed, demonstrating the concept of event horizons.
Carefully architected and organized, the application is divided into distinct classes and functions, each having clear, distinct responsibilities. From the calculations of distance and velocity to the creation and control of each space object, the application is a testament to a clean, modular, and scalable design. This project showcases my proficiency in C# programming, understanding of physics, and the ability to translate complex phenomena into an intuitive, visual medium. It's not just a great tool for learning and teaching, but also an impressive demonstration of bridging science and software development in an engaging way.
